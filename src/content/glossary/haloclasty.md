---
term: "Haloclasty"
greek: "ἅλς (hals, salt) + κλαστός (klastós, broken)"
definition: "Physical weathering caused by the growth and expansion of salt crystals inside rock pores and cracks. Common in coastal and arid environments where repeated wetting and drying with salt-rich water occurs."
etymology: "From Greek roots meaning 'salt' and 'broken'. Also called salt weathering or salt crystallization weathering."
notes: "In porous limestones such as Cypriot calcarenite, sea spray supplies sodium chloride and other salts. As water evaporates, crystals grow and exert pressure that exceeds the tensile strength of the rock, causing granular disintegration and the formation of tafoni."
