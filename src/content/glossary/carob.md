---
term: "Carob"
greek: "Χαρούπι (Charoúpi)"
definition: "The long, dark pod of the carob tree (Ceratonia siliqua), used for centuries as animal fodder, human food, and a source of natural sweetener in the Mediterranean."
etymology: "The word 'carat' (unit of gold purity and gem weight) comes from the Arabic qīrāṭ, which itself came from the Greek kerátion ('little horn'), referring to the carob seed which was used as a standard of weight because of its remarkable uniformity."
