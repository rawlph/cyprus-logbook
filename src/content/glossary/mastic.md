---
term: "Mastic"
greek: "Μαστίχα (Mastícha)"
definition: "The aromatic resin harvested from the mastic tree (Pistacia lentiscus). In Cyprus and the eastern Mediterranean it has been treasured for chewing, medicine, and varnish for thousands of years."
etymology: "From Greek μαστίχη, related to the verb 'to chew' or 'to gnash the teeth'."
notes: "The resin hardens into pale 'tears' when the bark is scored. It remains one of the few products still gathered by traditional methods in parts of the region."
