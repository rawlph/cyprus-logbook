---
term: "Koumandaria"
greek: "Κουμανδαρία"
definition: "A historic sweet wine of Cyprus, one of the oldest named wines still produced in the world. Made from sun-dried grapes, traditionally associated with the Commandaria region."
etymology: "Derives from 'Commanderie', referring to the estates of the Knights Templar and later the Knights of St John who controlled the area during the Crusader and Lusignan periods."
notes: "Richard the Lionheart is said to have praised it highly at his wedding on Cyprus in 1191."
---
