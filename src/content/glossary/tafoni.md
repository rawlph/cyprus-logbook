---
term: "Tafoni"
greek: "ταφόνι (tafóni, plural), τάφος (táphos, 'tomb' or 'sepulcher')"
definition: "Cavernous or honeycomb weathering features — rounded, hollowed-out cavities and alcoves that develop on rock surfaces, especially in coastal or arid settings."
etymology: "Mediterranean origin, still debated. Most often traced to Ancient Greek τάφος (táphos, 'tomb'), because the hollows look like small rock-cut niches or burial chambers. Alternative roots proposed in Corsican or Sicilian: taffoni ('windows') or tafonare ('to perforate'). The word entered geology in the late 19th century and is now used worldwide."
notes: "On the south coast of Cyprus, tafoni are spectacularly developed in the porous Pliocene calcarenite of the Athalassa Member through haloclasty. The holes are not random; they record the repeated work of salt crystals prying the rock apart grain by grain."
