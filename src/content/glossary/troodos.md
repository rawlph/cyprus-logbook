---
term: "Troodos"
greek: "Τρόοδος"
definition: "The central mountain range of Cyprus, formed from an ancient oceanic crust (ophiolite) that was thrust onto the island. Home to unique geology, forests, and many of the island’s painted Byzantine churches."
etymology: "Possibly from the ancient Greek τροῦς (trous) 'foot' or related to 'three roads' — the precise origin remains debated among scholars."
