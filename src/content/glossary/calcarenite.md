---
term: "Calcarenite"
greek: "From Latin calx (lime) + arena (sand)"
definition: "A type of limestone composed predominantly of sand-sized (0.0625–2 mm) detrital carbonate grains such as shell fragments, foraminifera, ooids, or pellets. It is the carbonate equivalent of a sandstone."
etymology: "Coined by Amadeus William Grabau in 1903 as part of his grain-size classification of limestones (calcilutite, calcarenite, calcirudite)."
notes: "In Cyprus the best-known examples belong to the Athalassa Member of the Nicosia Formation (Pliocene–early Pleistocene). These rocks were deposited as carbonate sands in a shallow seaway and are now famous for the tafoni that salt weathering has carved into them along the south coast."
