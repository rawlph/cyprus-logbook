---
term: "Athalassa Member"
greek: "Ἀθάλασσα (Athalassa, 'without sea' or a place name)"
definition: "A Pliocene to early Pleistocene member of the Nicosia Formation in Cyprus, consisting of cross-bedded, fossiliferous calcarenites and sandy marls deposited in a shallow marine seaway between the rising Troodos and Kyrenia landmasses."
etymology: "Named after the village of Athalassa near Nicosia; the name itself carries the resonance of land that was once submerged."
notes: "On the south coast of Cyprus the Athalassa calcarenites form prominent coastal cliffs and terraces. Roughly 4 million years old in many exposures, they are the very rocks in which the most dramatic examples of haloclasty and tafoni can be seen today."
